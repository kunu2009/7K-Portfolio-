## Common Mistakes (And How to Avoid Them)

### Mistake 1: Trying Everything

**Problem:** Offering 10 different services, mastering none.

**Solution:** Pick ONE skill. Get good. Then maybe expand.

### Mistake 2: Underpricing Forever

**Problem:** Staying at beginner rates even after gaining experience.

**Solution:** Raise rates every 3-5 projects. The right clients will pay.

### Mistake 3: Not Getting Testimonials

**Problem:** Great work, no social proof.

**Solution:** After EVERY project, ask for a testimonial. Make it easy for them.

### Mistake 4: Waiting to Be "Ready"

**Problem:** Taking courses forever, never starting.

**Solution:** Learn by doing. Apply with 70% knowledge, learn the 30% on the job.

### Mistake 5: Giving Up Too Soon

**Problem:** 2 weeks of applying, no response, quitting.

**Solution:** First client often takes 4-8 weeks. Consistency wins.

## The Mental Game

### Dealing with Rejection

You will be rejected. A lot. Especially initially.

**Reframe it:**
- Every rejection is practice
- Every "no" brings you closer to "yes"
- Most rejections aren't about youâ€”they're about fit/timing/budget

### Dealing with Low Rates Initially

Yes, you'll make less than minimum wage at first. That's okay.

**Why it's worth it:**
- You're learning on someone else's dime
- You're building portfolio
- You're building reputation
- Low rates now = high rates later

### Dealing with Imposter Syndrome

"Who am I to charge for this?"

**Remember:**
- Someone out there needs exactly your skill
- You're further along than complete beginners
- Clients pay for results, not certificates
- Everyone starts somewhere
